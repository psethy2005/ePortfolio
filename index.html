Intro

My name is Piyush Sethy, and I am a junior majoring in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Maryland, College Park. I am inspired by the efficient systems found in nature and driven to translate those principles into practical, data-driven engineering solutions. My work focuses on mechanical design, structural analysis, and prototyping, with applications in robotics and sustainable systems. I am particularly interested in designing reliable, scalable hardware that performs under real-world constraints. After graduation, I aim to work in robotics and sustainability to develop technologies that improve efficiency and contribute to a cleaner future. Below are selected projects showcasing my hands-on experience in design, analysis, and system integration.

LIGHTWEIGHT PHONE STAND DESIGN & STRUCTURAL ANALYSIS

  • Designed a compact phone stand under strict size (3 × 3 × 3 in³) and mass (≤ 0.045 lb) constraints
  • Developed Solidworks CAD model and performed FEA to evaluate stress distribution under expected loading
  • Optimized geometry by removing material from low-stress regions to reduce weight without structural failure
  • Evaluated stability in horizontal and vertical orientations and proposed design improvements
  • View Design Drawings & Analysis (PDF)

ESCALATOR SYSTEM DESIGN — STRUCTURAL SUBASSEMBLIES & ANALYSIS

  • Colloborated with a team of 7 members to create detailed part and assembly models of an escalator
  • Designed truss support and guide rail subassemblies for a full escalator system
  • Performed FEA on escalator stairs under simulated passenger loading (1200 N)
  • Conducted stress, displacement, and natural frequency analyses to assess safety and vibration behavior
  • View Assembly & FEA Documentation

ROBORAPTOR: ARTICULATED WING GLIDER

  • Designed and tested a bird-inspired articulated wing glider to study aerodynamic effects of flapping and gliding flight.
  • Iterated through four physical prototypes, improving wing articulation, materials, and structural integrity.
  • Integrated 3D-printed mounts, servo-actuated wings, and onboard electronics for controlled flapping motion.
  • Achieved stable flight in wind speeds above 7 mph using an eagle-inspired high-aspect-ratio wing design.
  • View Full Design & System Documentation (PDF)

AUTONOMOUS OTV FOR MATERIAL IDENTIFICATION

  • Led mechanical design of a robotic claw and to chassis for an autonomous OTV
  • Designed and 3d printed the rank-and-pinion claw and manufactured the chassis through laser-cutting
  • Innovatively used an ultrasonic sensor to distinguish material type based on signal scattering behavior
  • Calibrated and incorporated current sensors to distinguish current draw on the motor for different weights in order to classify them
  • View Full Design & System Documentation (PDF)

ACCESSIBILITY DESIGN PROJECT: BRAILLE FOR NATURE

  • Supervised a team of seven for the MIT CRE[AT]E Challenge, creating tactile braille signage to improve accessibility for visually impaired visitors along the Lewis and Clark trails
  • Designed and 3D-printed prototypes in an interative process by incorporating feedback users
  • Collaborated with students from the Montana School for the Deaf and Blind to refine the models, promoting an engaging and inclusive educational experience
